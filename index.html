in order.

02 — Housing & Roommates

Where you live

All freshmen with fewer than 27 credits are required to live on campus. Here's how the process works and what each area actually feels like.

01

Log into the Housing Portal and sign your Occupancy License starting June 1.

02

Complete the matching questionnaire — sleep schedule, noise, cleanliness, guests.

03

Found someone on Instagram or accepted students pages? Create a roommate group to be placed together.

04

Submit by June 30

The Five Residential Areas

Each area has a wildly different personality. Where you land shapes your social life freshman year.

🏙️

Southwest

"The biggest party on the East Coast"

The largest area with 5,500 students and high-rise towers. This is the most social area on campus — if you want to meet everyone, this is where it happens. Kennedy Hall is all-freshman and famously loud (don't plan on sleeping before 2 AM on weekends). Cance Hall is nicknamed "No Pants Cance" — enough said.

Party Scene High Energy 5,500 Students High-Rise Towers
📚

Northeast

"Smarter, still cool, pressure's off"

Closest area to the majority of academic buildings, so you can roll out of bed 10 minutes before class. Less party pressure than Southwest but still very social. People here tend to be described as "smarter, still cool" with the social pressure dialed back. Worcester Dining is right here with late-night hours.

Close to Classes Chill Vibe Late-Night Dining
⚡

Central

"Social but not over the top"

A solid middle ground — social but without Southwest's intensity. Gorman Hall is known for being a very social "popping" building. Central is a great choice if you want balance: enough energy to find your people, enough calm to actually study. Close to Franklin Dining (the best stir fry on campus).

Balanced Gorman Hall Near Franklin DC
🎨

Orchard Hill

"Artsy, hipster, tight-knit"

Isolated up a steep hill, O-Hill attracts the artsy and alternative crowd. Friend groups form fast and stay tight — but you might struggle to meet people outside your area unless you join clubs or Greek life. Beautiful views, unique community, and a bit of a hike to get anywhere.

Creative Crowd Tight Community Steep Hill
🌲

Sylvan

"The quiet one (suite-style)"

The most unknown and least social area. Primarily transfer students in suite-style housing. If you're an introvert who wants to avoid Southwest's chaos, this is your sanctuary. Suite setup is nice, but you'll need to be proactive about meeting people — join clubs and make the walk to campus social spots.

Suite-Style Quiet Introverts
03 — Academics & Isenberg

Isenberg doesn't
wait for you.

As a business school freshman, there are a few things that work differently for you. Get ahead of these before advising starts.

📐

Math Placement is Critical

For Isenberg students, the Math Placement Exam determines your path into required math and science courses. It's taken via the OWL system and can only be taken once — no retakes. Study beforehand and take it seriously.

Deadline: Complete all placement assessments by June 4, 2026
🗓️

Advising is Online

Between June 8 and July 24, you'll meet with an advisor via video call to review your placement results and register for fall classes. They'll contact you at your UMass email through Navigate. Activate your UMass email early so you don't miss it.

Check your UMass email starting June — advisors schedule through Navigate
📋

Gen Eds Matter More Than You Think

One of the biggest freshman mistakes is ignoring General Education requirements. These don't just fill your schedule — delaying them can push back your graduation date. Start knocking them out alongside your Isenberg pre-reqs from semester one.

🔓

Before Advising: 3 Prereqs

You must complete these before your advising appointment: (1) "Guide to the U" online module, (2) Register for "Ready for the U" orientation, (3) All placement assessments (Math, Writing, Foreign Language). Miss any of these and your advising will be delayed.

🏛️

RAPs: Academic Living Communities

Residential Academic Programs link your housing with a specific course — you live with your classmates and take a first-year class together. For Isenberg students, this means building relationships with peers in your major from day one. The RAP lottery opens May 19 with results on May 28. Most programs are open to all first-years, though some are invitation-only. Students in RAPs live in the Pierpont and Moore buildings in Southwest.

RAP Lottery: May 19 → Results: May 28 → Registration: June 1–30

05 — Making Friends

Nobody knows anyone.
That's the point.

Every single person around you during the first weeks is also looking for friends. The only way to lose is to stay in your room.

🎪

RSO Activity Fairs

UMass has hundreds of Registered Student Organizations. Show up to the activity fairs during Welcome Week and sign up for way more than you think you'll do — you can always drop later. These clubs are a massive resume booster for Isenberg students.

Pro tip: Sign up for 10+, narrow down to 3-4 that stick
🏠

Join a RAP

Residential Academic Programs place you in the same dorm and class with 20-30 peers. You eat together, study together, walk to class together. It's the fastest way to build a core friend group before the semester even starts.

Lottery opens May 19 — don't sleep on this
🎓

Greek Life

Fraternities and sororities are a popular way to expand your social and professional networks across campus. Especially useful if you're in Orchard Hill or Sylvan where organic socializing takes more effort.

Rush happens early fall — research chapters beforehand
🚪

Keep Your Door Open

Literally. During the first few weeks, leave your dorm room door open. People will walk by, peek in, introduce themselves. This is how floor friendships form. It feels awkward for about five minutes, then it becomes natural.

The first 2 weeks are when 80% of floor bonds form
📅

Welcome to the U

Orientation runs September 2–7 with campus tours, social events, and department mixers. Go to everything — even the stuff that sounds boring. You're not there for the event, you're there for the people sitting next to you.

Say yes to every invite the first month
⚖️

Work Hard, Play Hard

UMass has a strong social scene, but Isenberg is demanding. The students who thrive find the balance — prioritize grades during the week, be social on weekends. Burning out socially or academically are equally dangerous traps.

Your GPA matters for Isenberg progression — protect it
06 — Common Mistakes

Things freshmen
learn the hard way.

Every one of these came from students who went through it. You don't have to.

01

Skipping Gen Ed requirements

Delaying mandatory General Education courses is one of the most common reasons students don't graduate on time. Start early, even if they're not exciting.

02

Never going to office hours

Professors and TAs remember students who show up. These relationships become job references, research opportunities, and letters of recommendation. Isenberg is competitive — build these connections early.

03

Ignoring your UMass email

Your UMass email is the only way the university sends official updates — enrollment changes, financial notices, advising schedules. Not checking it is playing with fire.

04

Forgetting SPIRE holds & to-do's

SPIRE will block your class registration if you have unresolved holds or incomplete to-do items. Check it regularly — don't find out at 3 AM during course enrollment.

05

Using BitTorrent on campus Wi-Fi

Peer-to-peer file sharing for copyrighted material on the university network can lead to serious sanctions — including lawsuits. Just don't.

06

Underestimating Western Mass winter

The Amherst winter is no joke. Black ice is a real hazard, especially walking downtown. Invest in good boots, a real coat, and learn to layer. You'll thank yourself in February.
